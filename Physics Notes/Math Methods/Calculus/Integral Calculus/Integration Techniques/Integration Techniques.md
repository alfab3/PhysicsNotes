[[Integration by Substitution]]: 
$$\begin{align} \int f(g(x))g'(x)dx = \int f(u) du, \; \;u = g(x)\end{align}$$

[[Integration by Parts]]:
$$\begin{align} \int udv = uv - \int vdu\end{align}$$

[[Trigonometric substitution]]:
Cases:
1) $a^2-x^2$
2) $a^2+x^2$
3) $x^2-a^2$

[[Partial Fraction Decomposition]]:
In the form $$\begin{align} \frac{f(x)}{g(x)}=p(x)+\sum_j\frac{f_j(x)}{g_j(x)} \end{align}$$ where $p(x)$ is a polynomial, and, for each $j$, the denominator $g_j(x)$ is a power of an irreducible polynomial and the numerator $f_j(x)$ is a polynomial of a smaller degree than the degree of this ireducible polynomial.
